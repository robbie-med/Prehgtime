First Trimester
Weeks 1 – 13
Organogenesis · Highest teratogen risk · hCG peaks · Implantation & placentation
```
Wk 4–6
Appointment
Pregnancy Confirmation
First prenatal visit if not already seen. Confirm intrauterine pregnancy (IUP). Obtain LMP, EDD, obstetric history.
Labs
Initial Prenatal Panel
  • Type & Screen (ABO/Rh)
  • CBC (Hgb, Hct, platelets)
  • Rubella IgG titer
  • Varicella IgG titer
  • Hepatitis B sAg
  • HIV 1/2 Ag/Ab (4th gen)
  • RPR / VDRL (syphilis)
  • Gonorrhea & Chlamydia NAAT
  • Urine culture
  • Pap smear (if due)
  • TSH
  • Hgb A1c (if high risk)
  • Urine hCG (confirm)
Fetal Development
Implantation & Cardiac Poles
Blastocyst implants ~day 6–10. Cardiac tube forms; heart begins beating ~wk 5–6. Embryo ~4 mm CRL at wk 6.
Infections / TORCH
Critical Exposure Window
Rubella: T1 infection → congenital rubella syndrome (cataracts, cochlear defects, heart defects). CMV: Periventricular calcifications, hearing loss, chorioretinitis. Toxoplasma: Chorioretinitis, intracranial calcifications, hydrocephalus (if T1). Parvovirus B19: Hydrops fetalis via aplastic crisis of fetal RBCs. All TORCH risks highest in first trimester.
Treatments / Supplements
Early Supplementation
  • Folic acid: 0.4–0.8 mg/day (4 mg if prior NTD) — NTD prevention
  • Prenatal vitamin (iron, iodine, DHA)
  • Avoid teratogens: isotretinoin, valproate, warfarin, methotrexate, ACEi/ARBs
Wk 7–10
Screening / Imaging
Dating Ultrasound
Transvaginal U/S. Confirm IUP, viability, CRL for EDD confirmation. Assess for ectopic, molar, or multiple gestation. FHR documented (normal 110–160 bpm).
Labs
Quantitative β-hCG
Peaks at 8–10 weeks (~100,000 mIU/mL), then declines. Doubles every 48h in viable early pregnancy. Low/plateauing hCG → ectopic or failing IUP. Hyperemesis gravidarum correlated with high hCG.
Fetal Development
Organogenesis
All major organ systems forming. CNS: neural tube closes by day 28. Limbs, heart chambers, GI tract forming. Embryo becomes fetus at wk 10. CRL ~30 mm at wk 10.
Infections
HSV / Listeria
HSV: Intrauterine infection → microcephaly, hydrocephalus, vesicular lesions, very low birth weight (does NOT cause hydrops). Listeria: Asymptomatic mother; congenital listeriosis manifests at birth; maternal fever/GI symptoms may occur. Avoid unpasteurized dairy/deli meat.
Wk 10–13
Screening / Imaging
First Trimester Aneuploidy Screen
  • Nuchal translucency (NT) U/S: wk 11–13⁶⁄₇ — NT ≥3 mm → ↑ risk T21/T18/T13, cardiac defects
  • Cell-free fetal DNA (cfDNA/NIPT): ≥10 wks — T21, T18, T13, sex chromosome aneuploidies, optional microdeletions
  • Combined first trimester screen: NT + free β-hCG + PAPP-A
  • Low PAPP-A → FGR, preeclampsia risk
Labs
First Trimester Analytes
  • PAPP-A: Low → aneuploidy, FGR
  • Free β-hCG: High → T21; Low → T18/T13
  • Progesterone (if threatened AB)
Procedures
Diagnostic Options (if indicated)
  • CVS (Chorionic Villus Sampling): 10–13 wks — karyotype, microarray
  • Amniocentesis: deferred to T2 (15–20 wks)
Treatments
Preeclampsia Prophylaxis Risk Assessment
If high risk (prior preeclampsia, multifetal gestation, CKD, HTN, DM, autoimmune): initiate low-dose aspirin 81 mg/day starting 12–28 wks (ideally before 16 wks). Continue until 36–37 wks.
Fetal Development
End of Embryonic Period
Fetal period begins. External genitalia differentiate (may be visible on U/S 12–14 wks). Intestines return from umbilical stalk. CRL ~6 cm at 13 wks. Placenta functional.
```

Second Trimester
Weeks 14 – 27
Anatomic survey · Maternal serum screening · Fetal viability threshold · Gestational diabetes screen
```
Wk 14–16
Appointment
Routine Prenatal Visit
BP, weight, fundal height (FH = wks ± 2 cm), FHR doppler (120–160 bpm). Reassess symptoms: round ligament pain, leukorrhea, heartburn, constipation.
Labs
Quad Screen (MSAFP Panel)
  • AFP: ↑ → NTD, abdominal wall defects; ↓ → T21
  • hCG: ↑ → T21
  • Estriol (uE3): ↓ → T18, T21
  • Inhibin A: ↑ → T21
  • Performed 15–20 wks
Procedures
Amniocentesis (if indicated)
15–20 wks. Karyotype, FISH, microarray. Abnormal NIPT, advanced maternal age (≥35), or abnormal anatomy on U/S. Risk of pregnancy loss ~0.1–0.3%.
Infections
Parvovirus B19 Window
Parvovirus B19 (Fifth disease): Slapped-cheek rash, mild flu-like illness, polyarticular arthritis in adults. Fetal infection → aplastic crisis → severe anemia → nonimmune hydrops fetalis. Risk higher in T1–T2. No treatment; monitor with MCA Doppler (MCA-PSV >1.5 MoM → fetal anemia). Intrauterine transfusion if severe.
Wk 18–22
Imaging
Anatomy Survey Ultrasound
  • Fetal biometry: BPD, HC, AC, FL → EFW
  • Brain: lateral ventricles, cavum septum pellucidum, cerebellum, posterior fossa
  • Face: lip/palate, orbits, nasal bone
  • Heart: 4-chamber, LVOT, RVOT, 3-vessel view
  • Spine: neural tube integrity
  • Abdomen: stomach, kidneys, bladder, cord insertion
  • Placenta: location, umbilical cord (3 vessels)
  • Cervical length (if indicated: high risk for PTL)
  • Amniotic fluid index (AFI) or DVP
Fetal Development
Quickening & Organ Maturation
Mother feels fetal movement (quickening) 18–22 wks (earlier in multips). Fetal kidneys produce urine → amniotic fluid. Lungs begin surfactant production (type II pneumocytes). Lanugo present. Swallowing begins.
Labs
Fetal Anatomy Labs (Selected)
  • MCA-PSV Doppler if parvovirus exposure or alloimmunization
  • Maternal Hgb (anemia screen)
Infections
Rubella / VZV Late T2
Rubella >20 wks: hydrops fetalis rare. VZV (varicella): Congenital varicella syndrome — scar-like skin lesions, chorioretinitis, limb hypoplasia, cortical atrophy (detectable on antenatal U/S). VZV IgG negative mother → VZIG within 96h of exposure; acyclovir if active disease.
Wk 24–28
Labs
Gestational Diabetes Screening
  • 1-hr GCT (Glucose Challenge Test): 50g glucose load; threshold ≥130 or ≥140 mg/dL
  • If abnormal → 3-hr GTT (OGTT): 100g; Carpenter-Coustan criteria (fasting ≥95, 1h ≥180, 2h ≥155, 3h ≥140; 2+ values elevated = GDM)
  • Alternative: 2-hr 75g OGTT (IADPSG/WHO)
  • Repeat CBC (anemia)
  • Urine culture (asymptomatic bacteriuria)
  • Rh(D) antibody screen (28 wks)
Vaccinations
Tdap & Rh Immunoglobulin
  • Tdap vaccine: 27–36 wks (ideally 27–32) — protects newborn from pertussis via passive Ab transfer
  • Influenza: Any trimester (inactivated vaccine) during flu season
  • COVID-19: Any trimester (mRNA preferred)
  • RhoGAM (Rh immunoglobulin 300 μg IM): 28 wks if Rh(D)–; also after any bleeding episode, amniocentesis, trauma
Fetal Development
Viability Threshold
Fetal viability conventionally at 22–24 weeks. At 24 wks: ~50% survival with intensive NICU care. Surfactant production increases. Eyes open. Hearing present. Fetal weight ~600 g at 24 wks → ~1000 g at 28 wks.
Screening
Cervical Length & PTL Risk
Transvaginal cervical length: <25 mm at 24 wks → high risk preterm labor. 17-OHPC (17α-hydroxyprogesterone caproate): If prior spontaneous preterm birth <37 wks — weekly IM injections 16–36 wks. Vaginal progesterone 200 mg if short cervix.
```

Third Trimester
Weeks 28 – 40
Fetal growth · GBS screening · Antepartum surveillance · Delivery planning
```
Wk 28–32
Appointment
Every 2-Week Visits
BP, weight, FH (fundal height ≈ GA ± 2 cm), FHR auscultation. Assess for preeclampsia signs: headache, RUQ pain, visual changes, edema. Leopold maneuvers for lie/presentation.
Labs
Third Trimester Labs
  • CBC (repeat — anemia, thrombocytopenia)
  • Repeat HIV screen (high-risk or unknown status)
  • Repeat syphilis RPR (high-risk)
  • Repeat GC/Chlamydia (high-risk)
  • Hep B (if not done/negative prior)
  • Urine protein/creatinine ratio if pre-eclampsia concern
Imaging
Growth Ultrasound
Estimated fetal weight (EFW), BPP (biophysical profile), amniotic fluid. Umbilical artery Dopplers if FGR suspected. Placenta previa follow-up if low-lying at anatomy scan.
Fetal Development
Rapid Fetal Weight Gain
~200–250 g/week weight gain. Lung maturity increases. Vernix caseosa forms. Fetal position often vertex by 32 wks. Brain gyrification begins in earnest. 28 wks: ~1100 g; 32 wks: ~1800 g.
Treatments
Antenatal Corticosteroids
If preterm delivery anticipated <34 wks: Betamethasone 12 mg IM × 2 doses, 24h apart → promotes fetal lung maturity (surfactant), reduces RDS, IVH, NEC. Rescue course if >14 days since initial course and still <34 wks.
Wk 32–36
Surveillance
Antepartum Fetal Surveillance
  • Non-Stress Test (NST): 2 accelerations ≥15 bpm × 15 sec in 20 min → reactive. Done weekly or twice-weekly for high-risk (DM, HTN, FGR, post-dates)
  • BPP (Biophysical Profile): NST + U/S (breathing, movement, tone, AFI); max 10 pts; ≤6 → delivery consideration
  • Umbilical Artery Doppler: FGR workup; absent/reversed end-diastolic flow → severe compromise
Treatments
Magnesium Sulfate (Neuroprotection)
If delivery anticipated <32 wks (some protocols <34 wks): MgSO₄ 4–6 g IV load, then 1–2 g/hr × 12–24h → reduces risk of cerebral palsy. Monitor for toxicity: loss of DTRs (first sign), respiratory depression (Mg >12 mEq/L). Antidote: calcium gluconate 1 g IV.
Fetal Development
Lung Maturity & Positioning
By 34 wks: lungs nearly mature; surfactant sufficient for most. Lanugo diminishing. Eyes open. Grasp reflex present. 36 wks: ~2600 g. Head typically engaged in nullips.
Infections
GBS Screening & Late-Onset TORCH
  • Group B Strep (GBS) rectovaginal swab: 35–37 wks — universal screening
  • GBS+ → intrapartum penicillin G prophylaxis
  • Syphilis: Repeat RPR if high-risk; congenital syphilis prevented by treating mother with benzathine PCN G
  • HSV: Recurrent genital HSV → acyclovir suppression 36 wks until delivery; C-section if active lesions at onset of labor
Wk 37–40
Appointment
Weekly Visits — Term Planning
Weekly visits from 36 wks. Bishop score assessment (cervical ripening). Discuss delivery preferences, pain management, breastfeeding, newborn care. TOLAC counseling if prior cesarean.
Labs
Pre-delivery Labs
  • CBC (pre-op baseline)
  • Type & Screen (repeat if >72h from delivery)
  • Metabolic panel if preeclampsia
  • GBS result review
  • Coagulation studies if indicated (HELLP, placenta previa)
Fetal Development
Term Fetus
38 wks = early term; 39–40 wks = full term; 41 wks = late term; ≥42 wks = post-term. Average weight 3200–3500 g at 39–40 wks. Vernix, lanugo largely absent. Active fetal movement expected daily.
Induction Considerations
ARRIVE Trial / Indications
Elective induction at 39 wks (nullips): ARRIVE trial showed reduced C-section rate. Medical indications: GDM, HTN, FGR, post-term (41–42 wks), PROM. Cervical ripening: misoprostol, dinoprostone, Foley balloon, oxytocin.
Imaging
Post-dates Surveillance
≥41 wks: NST + AFI twice weekly. AFI <5 cm or DVP <2 cm → oligohydramnios → delivery. BPP if NST non-reactive.
```

Labor & Delivery
Intrapartum
Stages of labor · Fetal monitoring · GBS prophylaxis · Analgesia · Complications
```
Admission
Labs on Admission
Intrapartum Labs
  • CBC with differential
  • BMP / CMP
  • Type & Screen (crossmatch if high-risk)
  • GBS status review
  • Urine dipstick (protein, glucose)
  • Coagulation panel (if preeclampsia, abruption)
  • Group & hold (high-risk deliveries)
GBS Prophylaxis
Intrapartum Antibiotics
  • Penicillin G 5M units IV, then 2.5M units q4h (first-line, GBS+)
  • Ampicillin 2g IV, then 1g q4h (alternative)
  • Allergies: cefazolin (low-risk allergy); clindamycin or vancomycin if high-risk PCN allergy
  • Prophylaxis needed if: GBS+, unknown status + risk factors, prior GBS infant, GBS bacteriuria this pregnancy
Monitoring
Continuous EFM
Baseline FHR 110–160 bpm. Assess: variability (moderate = 6–25 bpm, reassuring), accelerations (reassuring), decelerations (early → head compression; variable → cord; late → uteroplacental insufficiency). Category I/II/III classification.
Stages of Labor
Stage 1 — Latent Phase
Cervical Effacement & Dilation
0–6 cm. Nullips: latent up to 20h; multips: up to 14h. Irregular contractions → regular. Bishop score guides induction. Arrest of latent phase = prolonged if >20h (nullips) before active labor.
Stage 1 — Active Phase
6 cm → Complete
6–10 cm. Expected dilation ≥1 cm/hr (ACOG 2014: active phase begins at 6 cm). Arrest: <0.5 cm/2h with adequate contractions. Adequate contractions: ≥200 MVUs on IUPC or ≥3 ctx/10 min.
Analgesia
Labor Pain Management
  • Epidural: 0.0625–0.125% bupivacaine ± fentanyl; gold standard; may slow labor minimally
  • CSE (combined spinal-epidural): Faster onset
  • IV opioids: Fentanyl 50–100 μg IV; butorphanol; avoid near delivery (neonatal respiratory depression)
  • Nitrous oxide (50/50): Inhaled; modest effect
Stage 2
Pushing → Delivery
Full dilation to delivery. Nullips without epidural: <2h; with epidural: <3h. Multips: <1h/2h. Arrest of descent: no fetal descent after 1h adequate pushing (nullip). Operative delivery options: vacuum, forceps. C-section if failed.
Stage 3
Placental Delivery
Delivery of infant → delivery of placenta. Normally ≤30 min. Signs of separation: gush of blood, umbilical cord lengthening, fundal rise. Active management: oxytocin 10 units IM immediately after delivery → reduces PPH risk.
Complications
Intrapartum Infections
Chorioamnionitis (IAI)
  • Fever ≥39°C OR 38–39°C ×2 (30 min apart) + maternal tachycardia, fetal tachycardia, uterine tenderness, foul-smelling fluid
  • Treatment: Ampicillin 2g IV q6h + Gentamicin 1.5 mg/kg q8h (or 5mg/kg q24h)
  • Add clindamycin or metronidazole if C-section
  • Fetal tachycardia >160 bpm → early sign
Complications — Labs
HELLP Syndrome
  • Hemolysis (schistocytes, ↑LDH >600, ↑indirect bili)
  • Elevated Liver enzymes (AST/ALT >70)
  • Low Platelets (<100k)
  • Management: MgSO₄ seizure prophylaxis, antihypertensives (labetalol, hydralazine, nifedipine), delivery
  • Plt <50k → transfuse if C-section; <20k → transfuse any delivery
PPH Prevention
Postpartum Hemorrhage Protocol
  • Oxytocin 10–40 units in 1L NS IV (first-line)
  • Methylergonovine 0.2 mg IM (avoid if HTN)
  • Carboprost (15-methyl PGF2α) 0.25 mg IM q15–90 min (avoid asthma)
  • Misoprostol 800–1000 μg rectally
  • Tranexamic acid 1g IV (within 3h of delivery)
  • Uterine massage, bimanual compression, balloon tamponade
```

Postpartum
0 – 6 Weeks
Maternal recovery · Newborn care · Vaccinations · Mental health · Lactation
```
Immediate (0–24h)
Newborn Labs
Neonatal Assessments
  • APGAR score: 1 min and 5 min (A-appearance, P-pulse, G-grimace, A-activity, R-respiration; 7–10 = normal)
  • Cord blood gases: pH, pCO2, base excess
  • Cord blood type & Coombs (if Rh– mother)
  • Newborn blood glucose (if DM, macrosomia, SGA)
  • Pulse oximetry screen (CCHD) at 24h
Newborn Prophylaxis
Immediate Newborn Treatments
  • Vitamin K1 1 mg IM: VKDB prevention (hemorrhagic disease of newborn)
  • Erythromycin 0.5% ophthalmic ointment: GC/chlamydia ophthalmia prophylaxis
  • Hepatitis B vaccine (HepB1): Within 12–24h if HepBsAg– mom; <12h if HepBsAg+ mom + HBIG 0.5 mL IM
Newborn
Neonatal Hyperbilirubinemia
Physiologic jaundice peaks day 3–5 (indirect bili). Pathologic if <24h (hemolytic — ABO, Rh incompatibility). Bhutani nomogram: plot serum bili vs. age in hours → risk stratification. Phototherapy threshold varies by GA and risk factors. Exchange transfusion if severe.
Maternal
4th Stage of Labor
First 1–2h postpartum. Monitor: uterine tone/fundus, lochia, BP, HR, pain, bladder. PPH: blood loss >1000 mL (or any hemodynamically significant loss). VS q15 min × 1h, then q30 min × 1h.
Day 1–3 (Inpatient)
Newborn Screening
Newborn State Screens
  • NBS (newborn metabolic screen): Heel stick at 24–48h — PKU, hypothyroidism, galactosemia, CAH, MCAD, hemoglobinopathies, and 30+ others (state-specific)
  • Hearing screen (AABR/OAE): Universal; before discharge
  • CCHD screen: Pulse ox right hand + right foot; repeat if fail
  • Repeat NBS at 1–2 wks if initial done <24h
Maternal Labs
Postpartum Maternal Labs
  • CBC (Hgb/Hct — if significant blood loss)
  • Serum bili if GBS+ and neonatal jaundice
  • Thyroid function (TSH) — screen for postpartum thyroiditis at 4–6 wks
  • RhoGAM 300 μg IM within 72h if Rh– mom & Rh+ or unknown baby
Postpartum Infections
Endometritis & Wound Infection
  • Endometritis: Fever + uterine tenderness + foul lochia; Rx: clindamycin 900mg IV q8h + gentamicin 1.5mg/kg q8h; C/S higher risk
  • Wound infection: Cellulitis vs. abscess; open and drain; antibiotics
  • Mastitis: Unilateral breast tenderness, erythema, flu-like sx; dicloxacillin or cephalexin × 10–14 days; continue breastfeeding
Lactation
Breastfeeding Support
Colostrum days 1–3; transitional milk days 3–14; mature milk thereafter. Latch assessment by lactation consultant. Adequate feeds: 8–12 times/day; 6 wet diapers/day by day 5. WHO recommends exclusive BF × 6 months. Medications compatible with BF: most antibiotics, labetalol, nifedipine, most antidepressants (sertraline safest).
Wk 2–6
Appointments
Postpartum Follow-up
  • OB visit 2–3 wks (C-section wound check, BP recheck if hypertensive)
  • Comprehensive postpartum visit 6 wks: ACOG recommends contact within 3 wks + comprehensive at 12 wks postpartum
  • Newborn visits: 3–5 days, 2 wks, 1 month (per AAP schedule)
Mental Health
Postpartum Depression Screening
Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) at 1-month and 6-month visits. Score ≥10 → screen further; ≥13 → likely PPD. Baby blues: Normal, days 3–10, transient. PPD: Onset within 4 wks–1 year; SSRI (sertraline, escitalopram) safe with BF; therapy. Postpartum psychosis: Rare, emergency; hallucinations, delusions.
Contraception
Postpartum Contraception
  • Progestin-only pill (POP): Safe while BF; start immediately postpartum
  • Combined OCP: Avoid <6 wks (thrombosis risk); <6 months if exclusively BF (estrogen suppresses lactation)
  • LARC (IUD/implant): Immediate postpartum IUD (within 10 min of placental delivery) or 4+ wks; implant any time
  • Depo-Provera: Any time postpartum
Labs at 6 Weeks
6-Week Postpartum Panel
  • GDM → 75g OGTT at 4–12 wks postpartum
  • TSH (postpartum thyroiditis)
  • CBC if persistent fatigue/anemia
  • Hgb A1c + fasting glucose (GDM patients long-term)
  • Pap smear if deferred
```

TORCH Infections — Quick Reference

Transplacental fetal infections organized by pathogen, transmission, fetal effects, and management

T — Toxoplasma gondii
TransmissionCat feces, raw meat, soil
Maternal sxUsually asymptomatic; lymphadenopathy, fever
Fetal effectsChorioretinitis, intracranial calcifications, hydrocephalus (T1); "blueberry muffin" rash
DxIgM/IgG serology; PCR amniotic fluid
RxPyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin
Hydrops?Rare
O — Parvovirus B19
TransmissionRespiratory droplets (highly contagious)
Maternal sxSlapped-cheek rash, polyarticular arthritis, flu-like illness
Fetal effectsAplastic crisis → severe anemia → nonimmune hydrops fetalis
DxIgM/IgG; PCR; MCA-PSV Doppler >1.5 MoM
RxIntrauterine transfusion if hydrops; no vaccine
Hydrops?YES
O — Other: Syphilis (T. pallidum)
TransmissionSexual; transplacental (any trimester)
Maternal sxPainless chancre → condylomata lata → neurosyphilis
Fetal effectsCongenital syphilis: bone lesions, rash, hepatomegaly, CNS, saddle nose — manifests months after birth
DxRPR (screen) + FTA-ABS or TPPA (confirm)
RxBenzathine penicillin G (only proven treatment in pregnancy)
Hydrops?Rare
O — Other: VZV (Varicella)
TransmissionAirborne/contact; highly contagious
Maternal sxPruritic vesicular rash, fever, malaise; pneumonia risk in pregnancy
Fetal effectsScar-like lesions, chorioretinitis, limb hypoplasia, cortical atrophy (no hydrops)
DxVZV IgG (immune status); DFA from vesicle
RxVZIG within 96h exposure (if IgG–); acyclovir if active disease
Hydrops?NO
R — Rubella
TransmissionRespiratory droplets
Maternal sxFlu-like prodrome, post-auricular LAD, maculopapular rash
Fetal effectsCongenital rubella syndrome: cataracts, cochlear defects, heart defects (PDA, VSD). Highest risk T1 (>20 wks = rare hydrops)
DxRubella IgM/IgG; rubella IgG avidity test
RxNo treatment; MMR vaccine postpartum (live = contraindicated in pregnancy)
Hydrops?Rarely (<20 wk)
C — CMV
TransmissionContact with body fluids, sexual, breast milk
Maternal sxAsymptomatic (majority) or CMV mononucleosis (fever, malaise, sore throat)
Fetal effectsCNS abnormalities, hearing loss, chorioretinitis, periventricular calcifications, oligohydramnios, hydrops
DxU/S: periventricular calcifications; PCR amniotic fluid; maternal CMV IgM/IgG avidity
RxValacyclovir (investigational); CMV hyperimmune globulin (some centers); no vaccine
Hydrops?YES
H — HSV (Herpes simplex)
TransmissionContact with active lesions; vertical transmission at delivery
Maternal sxPainful vesicles/ulcerations on genitals or lips (may be asymptomatic)
Fetal effectsMicrocephaly, hydrocephalus, microphthalmia, vesicular lesions, very low birth weight (no hydrops)
DxPCR swab from vesicle; HSV-1/2 IgG serology
RxAcyclovir suppression from 36 wks; C-section if active lesions at labor onset
Hydrops?NO
O — Other: Listeria
TransmissionContaminated food (deli meat, soft cheeses, unpasteurized dairy)
Maternal sxAsymptomatic or fever, headache, GI symptoms; septicemia
Fetal effectsGranulomatosis infantiseptica; congenital listeriosis (cannot be diagnosed antenatally)
DxBlood/CSF cultures; maternal symptoms key
RxAmpicillin + gentamicin; TMP-SMX in penicillin allergy
Hydrops?NO

Reference Lab Values in Pregnancy

Normal physiologic changes alter many lab values — interpret in obstetric context

Hematology
Hgb↓ 10–12 g/dL (hemodilution; non-preg 12–16)
Hct30–36% (↓)
WBC↑ 9–16 × 10³ (normal; up to 20–25k in labor)
PlateletsNormal or slight ↓; gestational thrombocytopenia ~6% (Plt 70–150)
MCVNormal 80–100 fL; microcytic → iron deficiency (most common anemia in pregnancy)
Ferritin↓ (<10–15 µg/L → iron deficiency); iron supplements if <30
Chemistry
Na↓ 132–140 mEq/L (osmostat reset)
BUN / Cr↓ (↑ GFR 40–65%); Cr <0.8 mg/dL normal in pregnancy
Albumin↓ 2.5–3.5 g/dL (hemodilution)
Alk Phos↑↑ 2–4× (placental isoenzyme — not liver)
AST/ALTNormal; elevation → HELLP, acute fatty liver, cholestasis
Glucose↓ Fasting (insulin sensitivity ↑ T1); insulin resistance ↑↑ T2–T3
Total CO2/HCO3↓ ~18–21 mEq/L (compensatory metabolic acidosis for respiratory alkalosis)
Coagulation
PT / aPTTSlightly shortened (hypercoagulable state); PT normal or ↓
Fibrinogen↑↑ 400–600 mg/dL (non-preg 200–400); ↓ in DIC, abruption
D-dimer↑ progressively through pregnancy (not useful for DVT/PE diagnosis in pregnancy without clinical pretest)
vWF / Factor VIII↑ — protect against VWD symptoms during pregnancy
Hormones
β-hCGPeaks 8–10 wks (~100,000 mIU/mL) → plateau T2–T3
Progesterone↑↑ throughout (corpus luteum → placenta by 8–10 wks)
Estradiol↑↑↑ (1000× non-pregnant levels at term)
TSH↓ T1 (hCG cross-reacts with TSH receptor); free T4 ↑ slightly T1. Use trimester-specific ranges
Cortisol↑ (physiologic hypercortisolism of pregnancy)
Urinalysis
Protein<300 mg/24h normal; ≥300 → proteinuria (preeclampsia threshold)
GlycosuriaMay occur normally (↑ GFR exceeds tubular reabsorption); not diagnostic of DM alone
Urine cultureScreen for asymptomatic bacteriuria at first visit; treat if ≥100k CFU/mL → prevents pyelonephritis & PTL
Pulmonary / ABG
Respiratory rate↑ tidal volume (not RR); progesterone-driven → compensated respiratory alkalosis
pCO2↓ ~30–32 mmHg (chronic hyperventilation)
pO2↑ slightly (~105 mmHg)
pH7.40–7.47 (mild alkalosis compensated by renal HCO3 excretion)
FRC↓ 20% (uterus elevation); increased risk hypoxia with apnea (rapid desaturation)
